What it does

A system, not a motivation app

Built after understanding the theory behind sleep timing but finding that understanding alone wasn't enough. The key insight: small, visible improvement is more powerful than big challenges.

  • Daily wake-up time logging — the only input required to start the habit
  • Streak tracking to build consistency and make missing a day feel significant
  • Wake-up history and trend chart showing gradual shift over weeks

Personal result: shifted from waking at 10 AM to consistently waking at 6 AM by improving 10–20 minutes every few days.

Patent

Filed and pending grant.

Application Filed · CIPC South Africa

A Multi-Model Understanding and Quality Based Assessment System for Programming Education

Application No. 2026/05471
Filed 21 May 2026
Classification G09B
Abstract

Learning programming algorithms is hard for beginners because of the gap between theory and implementation, and most existing tools overload students with features that distract from the actual learning goal. This filing introduces MMUQ (Multi-Model Understanding and Quality), an assessment model built around a simpler idea: give students relevant practice problems to build a base, hands-on hints instead of passive visualizations, and continuous feedback as they go.

The platform tests a student's actual code, scores their written explanations using generative AI, and surfaces an analytics dashboard tracking conceptual versus practical understanding over time — turning programming education into a feedback loop rather than a one-shot lecture.
